The solar system

Eight planets, one shared star.

Every planet here orbits the Sun, and nothing else, which is the modern definition that quietly retired Pluto in 2006. Ordered by distance from the Sun, closest first.

01 · CLOSEST TO THE SUN

Mercury

A scorched, cratered world with almost no atmosphere to hold in heat.

Diameter4,879 km
Day length59 d
Moons0
02 · MORNING & EVENING STAR

Venus

Thick clouds of sulfuric acid trap heat, making it the hottest planet despite not being closest to the Sun.

Diameter12,104 km
Surface temp465°C
Moons0
03 · HOME WORLD

Earth

The only planet known to host liquid water on its surface and life of any kind.

Diameter12,742 km
Day length24 h
Moons1
04 · THE RED PLANET

Mars

Iron oxide dust gives Mars its colour. Dry riverbeds hint at a wetter past.

Diameter6,779 km
Year length687 d
Moons2
05 · THE GIANT

Jupiter

The largest planet in the solar system, with a storm — the Great Red Spot — wider than Earth.

Diameter139,820 km
Day length9.9 h
Moons95+
06 · THE RINGED ONE

Saturn

Famous for its rings made of ice and rock, some pieces as small as dust and others the size of houses.

Diameter116,460 km
Year length29.5 yr
Moons140+
07 · THE TILTED ICE GIANT

Uranus

Rotates almost on its side, so each pole gets 42 years of continuous sunlight, then 42 years of dark.

Diameter50,724 km
Year length84 yr
Moons27+
08 · FARTHEST FROM THE SUN

Neptune

Windiest planet in the solar system, with storms clocked at over 2,000 km/h.

Diameter49,244 km
Year length165 yr
Moons14+
